Regulatory Landscape

CAPSA CAP Guidelines — Updated 2024

The first major revision in 20 years signals a fundamental shift: from simply offering investment options to actively helping members achieve better retirement outcomes.

🎯

Outcomes over options

The old guidelines focused on giving members investment choices. The 2024 update shifts to retirement income adequacy — sponsors must now consider whether their plan actually helps members retire well, not just invest.

📊

Expanded scope

Now covers VRSPs, LIFs, RRIFs, and TFSAs — not just traditional CAPs. Applies to both the accumulation and decumulation phases, recognizing that retirement planning doesn't end at contribution.

⚡

Auto-features & defaults

Explicitly encourages auto-enrollment, auto-escalation, and balanced default options. Plans with auto-enrollment see 90%+ participation vs. 30-40% for voluntary.

🧭

Decision-making support

Sponsors should provide members with retirement income projections, financial planning tools, and access to professional advice — not just fund fact sheets.

Why this is a massive opportunity for advisors

  • Employers need expert guidance to meet new governance standards
  • Plan sponsors must now review investment options, fees, and member outcomes regularly
  • The shift to outcomes-based thinking creates ongoing advisory relationships, not one-time sales
  • Advisors become the compliance bridge between regulators and small employers
